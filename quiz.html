What is your curl type?

How to find your curl type

Step 1 — Wash it. The only way to see your real curl pattern is on clean, product-free hair. Build-up, heat damage, and old product all change how the curl sits.

Wet vs dry: Your curl pattern is visible when wet. 3A–3C hair stays loose and springy when dry. 4A–4C hair shrinks dramatically when dry — what looks like tight coils wet may look like a dense afro when dry. Look at it wet to type it.

Quick guide:

  • —3A: Loose spirals, about the size of a finger. Shiny when wet.
  • —3B: Springy, tight curls. Still holds definition when dry.
  • —3C: Dense corkscrews, lots of volume. Shrinks noticeably when dry.
  • —4A: Tight coils. Pull one strand — you can see a faint S or Z pattern.
  • —4B: Z-pattern coil. No visible curl when dry. Looks almost flat without product.
  • —4C: No visible pattern at all. Highest shrinkage. The most common texture across West Africa.

If you're still unsure, choose 4C — it's the most common type and its recommendations are the most broadly applicable for West African hair.
