    Scent Profile

    Your climate, your scent

    How to apply — Products & Technique

    ◈
    Perfume / EDP
    Eau de Parfum · 15–20% oil

    The most concentrated and longest-lasting format. 1–2 sprays maximum — EDP is strong and heat amplifies it significantly in Nigerian climates. Apply to pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears. Do not rub wrists together — it crushes the top notes and shortens longevity. Let it dry on the skin.

    ◉
    Cologne / EDT
    Eau de Toilette · 5–15% oil

    Lighter concentration, shorter projection (2–4 hours in heat). The more practical daily format for Lagos, Port Harcourt, and Accra. 2–3 sprays is the standard — spray at the chest and one wrist. Reapply midday rather than overshooting in the morning. In cool or dry climates, spray distance from skin matters: hold 15–20cm away.

    ◷
    Body Spray
    1–3% oil · Water & alcohol base

    Not a perfume substitute — a light, all-over freshness layer. Apply after showering to the whole body: chest, arms, neck. It fades within 1–2 hours and is meant to be reapplied. Works best as a base layer under a light EDT for longer wear. Not appropriate as your sole scent for evening occasions — too faint.

    ◌
    Deodorant & Roll-On
    Sweat control · Not fragrance

    A different function entirely — sweat and odour control, not scent. Apply to clean, dry underarms only. Roll-ons sit directly on the skin; sprays cover a wider area. Apply at night before bed for best anti-perspirant effect — the active ingredient (aluminium) works while you sleep. In the morning, reapply a light layer. Do not spray deodorant on clothes — it stains and does not work properly.

    Pulse Points
    Wrists, neck, behind the ears, inside elbows, behind the knees. These spots generate heat which activates and projects the fragrance. Focus on 2–3 points, not all of them at once — layering on every point amplifies into something overwhelming in heat.
    Don't rub
    Rubbing wrists together after applying breaks the top note molecules and shortens how long the fragrance lasts. Spray and leave it. This is one of the most common mistakes.
    Moisturise first
    Fragrance lasts significantly longer on moisturised skin. Apply an unscented body lotion first, then your fragrance on top. Dry skin absorbs and burns off scent faster — which is why heavily moisturised skin always seems to hold fragrance better.
    Layering
    Body spray first as a base → deodorant on underarms → EDT or EDP on pulse points. This order builds up correctly. The body spray extends the base layer, the deodorant handles sweat, and the perfume sits on top as the final scent identity. Never spray two different perfumes on top of each other unless they are from the same line.
    Heat & climate
    In high humidity, scent projects more aggressively — apply less than you think you need. In dry climates, fragrance evaporates faster — slightly more is appropriate.
    On clothes
    Spraying on fabric makes fragrance last longer but can stain — especially EDP concentrations on light fabrics. Safe on the inside of collars or scarves. Never spray directly onto silk or dry-clean-only fabric.

    Deodorant and perfume are not competing — they are different tools. Deodorant controls sweat odour at the source. Perfume is your scent identity. Both should always be present, never mistaken for each other.
